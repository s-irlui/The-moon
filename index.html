Welcome to the Moon

Here’s a short, simple story for you, --- The Moon’s Secret Every night, Amani would sit by her window and look up at the moon. It always seemed to follow her, glowing softly like it had something to say. One evening, she whispered, “Why do you follow me?” To her surprise, the moon flickered and replied, “I don’t follow you… I watch over you.” Amani blinked in shock. “Watch over me? Why?” “Because you once wished for light when your world felt dark,” the moon said gently. “I heard you.” Amani remembered that night—when everything felt heavy and she had quietly asked for hope. “Since then,” the moon continued, “I shine a little brighter just for you.” From that day on, Amani never felt alone. No matter where she went, the moon was there—silent, glowing, and keeping her secret safe. And high above, the moon smiled… because someone finally knew it was listening.

About the Moon

The moon is Earth's only natural satellite and is the fifth largest moon in the solar system. It has a diameter of about 3,474 kilometers and is located approximately 384,400 kilometers away from Earth. The moon's gravitational pull is responsible for the tides on Earth, and it has been a source of inspiration for countless cultures throughout history.
